Pasta Recipe

Description

Pasta is a staple food in Italian cuisine and is loved by people worldwide. It's made from durum wheat flour or semolina and water, mixed into a dough, shaped into various forms, and then cooked by boiling. There are numerous types of pasta, each with its own unique shape, texture, and culinary uses.


Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water (as needed)

Steps:

  1. On a clean work surface, mound the flour and create a well in the center.
  2. Crack the eggs into the well and add salt.
  3. Using a fork, gradually incorporate the flour into the eggs until a dough forms.
  4. If the dough is too dry, add a little water, a teaspoon at a time, until the dough comes together. If it's too sticky, add a bit more flour.
  5. Knead the dough for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  6. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes.
  7. Dust your work surface and rolling pin with flour.
  8. Take one portion of dough and flatten it slightly with your hands.
  9. Roll out the dough into a thin sheet, about 1/8 inch thick, using the rolling pin. Dust with more flour as needed to prevent sticking.
  10. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  11. Carefully add the pasta to the boiling water and cook according to the package instructions or until al dente (cooked but still firm to the bite).
  12. Fresh pasta usually cooks much faster than dried pasta, typically within 1-3 minutes depending on the thickness.
  13. Drain the cooked pasta and toss it with your favorite sauce and toppings.
