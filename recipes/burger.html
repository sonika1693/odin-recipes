Burger Recipe

Description

A burger, short for hamburger, is a popular sandwich consisting of a cooked patty of ground meat placed between two slices of bread (usually a bun). Burgers are often served with various toppings and condiments, making them a versatile and customizable meal.


Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef (preferably 80/20 or 85/15 lean-to-fat ratio)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 burger buns
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Tomato slices
  • Onion slices
  • Pickle slices
  • Cheese slices (optional)
  • Ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise (or your preferred condiments)
  • Cooking oil or butter for grilling

Steps:

  1. Preheat your grill, stovetop griddle, or skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Divide the ground beef into four equal portions and gently shape them into patties, about 1/2 to 3/4 inch thick. Make an indentation in the center of each patty with your thumb to prevent it from puffing up while cooking.
  3. Season both sides of each patty generously with salt and pepper.
  4. If using a grill, brush the grates with oil to prevent sticking. Place the patties on the grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare to medium doneness, or longer if you prefer well-done burgers. Avoid pressing down on the patties with a spatula as it can release juices and dry out the meat.
  5. If using a stovetop griddle or skillet, heat some oil or butter and cook the patties for the same amount of time per side until they reach your desired level of doneness.
  6. While the patties are cooking, lightly toast the burger buns on the grill or in a separate skillet until they are golden brown.
  7. Spread ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise, or your preferred condiments on the bottom half of each bun.
  8. Place a lettuce leaf on top of the sauce.
  9. Add a tomato slice, onion slice, and pickle slices on top of the lettuce.
  10. If using cheese, place a slice on each cooked patty during the last minute of cooking to melt.
  11. Place the cooked patty on top of the toppings.
  12. Complete the burger by placing the top half of the bun over the patty.
